About

Hi, I am Jinpeng Lu, a master's student in Information and Communication Engineering at the University of Science and Technology of China. I work with Prof. Zhiwei Xiong.

My research centers on multimodal representation learning for video-language models, world models, and multimodal medical imaging. I am especially interested in how models build representations that support long-horizon state tracking, spatial reasoning, and reliable perception.

My earlier and ongoing work in Medical AI focuses on efficient 3D medical segmentation and foundation-model transfer, and I now extend the same emphasis on rigorous evaluation and efficient systems to world models and Video MLLMs. My recent work includes WRBench for persistent-state diagnosis, VeloxSeg for efficient multimodal 3D segmentation, and medical foundation-model evaluation with DINOv3.

I am interested in building world models with robust, persistent state cores, as well as video-language models with long-term memory and spatial awareness.

Research

Current interests organized around world state, action, and efficient perception.

World Model Evaluation Persistent state, observability, and counterfactual camera interventions.

I study whether video generation systems maintain hidden object states beyond visible pixels. In WRBench, camera motion is treated as an observability intervention: a model must leave a scene, allow hidden events to evolve, and return with state-consistent evidence rather than only plausible pixels.

This line focuses on benchmark design, human-calibrated evaluation, public artifact release, and failure analysis across model families, camera-control interfaces, and long-horizon visual consistency.

Embodied Intelligence and VLA Models Representation tracing, action routing, and unified understanding-generation-action loops.

I am interested in how multimodal representations become embodied behavior. My recent collaborations include VLA-Trace, which links layer-level representation dynamics to rollout behavior, and Pelican-Unify, which connects understanding, reasoning, future imagination, and action prediction.

The goal is to make VLA and embodied foundation models more inspectable: where task evidence appears, how it propagates, and when it fails to control action.

Medical Vision Foundation Models Efficient 3D perception, multimodal segmentation, and transfer limits.

My earlier work develops efficient and robust segmentation methods for PET/CT and 3D medical images. In VeloxSeg, I combine JL-guided convolution, paired window attention, and spatially decoupled knowledge transfer for lightweight multimodal 3D segmentation.

I also study transfer limits of visual foundation models in medicine, including DINOv3-style self-supervised features across 2D/3D classification, segmentation, and registration tasks.

Research Threads

Broader questions behind the paper list; the publications above provide formal records and source figures.

Persistent State in World Models

Question. Can current video/world models preserve hidden world state after the camera leaves, events evolve, and the view returns?

Approach. I designed WRBench around three separable checks: camera-interaction following, visible-scene continuity, and returned-object state consistency.

  • 23 evaluated models across four control paradigms.
  • 9,600 generated videos with state-return diagnostics.
  • Failure modes: frozen hidden events, access-direction sensitivity, and metric paradoxes.

Embodied Representation to Action

Question. How do multimodal representations become action, and where can reasoning or state tracking break before rollout behavior fails?

Approach. I contribute to representation- and behavior-level analysis for embodied intelligence, focusing on how evidence is routed into action predictions.

  • Layer-wise representation tracing and attention-behavior alignment.
  • Rollout-level probes for vision-language-action models.
  • Unified understanding-generation-action framing in embodied foundation models.

Efficient 3D Medical Vision

Question. How can 3D medical systems improve robustness and transfer while keeping the model light enough for high-dimensional multimodal imaging?

Approach. My medical vision work targets the accuracy-efficiency tension through lightweight 3D modules, hierarchical PET/CT fusion, and foundation-model transfer analysis.

  • VeloxSeg: 1.66M parameters with 11x GPU and 48x CPU throughput gains over baseline settings.
  • H2ASeg: hierarchical adaptive PET/CT interaction for tumor segmentation.
  • DINOv3 medical benchmark: transfer analysis across 2D/3D tasks.
